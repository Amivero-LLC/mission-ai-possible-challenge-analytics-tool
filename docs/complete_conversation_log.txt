[Message 2] ASSISTANT:
A unicorn is typically described as:

**Basic form:** A horse-like creature, usually white or pale in color

**The horn:** A single, spiraled horn (called an "alicorn") projecting from the center of its forehead

**Physical features:**
- Often depicted as more delicate and graceful than regular horses
- Sometimes shown with a flowing, silky mane and tail
- May have cloven hooves (like a goat) rather than horse hooves in some traditions
- Often portrayed with a beard-like tuft of hair on the chin

**Variations across cultures:**
- Medieval European art often showed them as goat-like with a beard
- Modern depictions usually make them more horse-like and ethereal
- Some versions include feathered wings (though that's technically a "winged unicorn" or alicorn)
- Colors can vary in fantasy art - rainbow, silver, or other magical hues

The unicorn's appearance has evolved significantly over time, from the more goat-like creatures in medieval tapestries to the elegant, pure white horses commonly seen in modern fantasy art and children's books.
